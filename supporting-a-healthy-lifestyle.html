Alcohol Advice

Alcohol — Know Your Limits

The NHS advises that both men and women should not regularly drink more than 14 units of alcohol per week — and if you do drink that much, to spread it over 3 or more days rather than consuming it all at once. "Regularly" means drinking this amount most weeks.

🍺 How Many Units Is That?

  • A pint of regular-strength beer (4%) ≈ 2.3 units
  • A large glass of wine 250ml (13%) ≈ 3.25 units
  • A single measure of spirits 25ml (40%) ≈ 1 unit
  • A bottle of wine ≈ 9–10 units
  • The 14-unit weekly guideline = roughly 6 pints of average-strength lager
  • Regularly exceeding limits raises risk of liver disease, cancer, heart disease and stroke
  • Alcohol can affect sleep, mental health and weight
  • Aim for several alcohol-free days each week
  • If you're concerned about your drinking, our GPs can help — confidentially
💬
Speak to your GP about cutting down. Free support is also available via Drinkaware.co.uk and NHS alcohol support services.
Smoking Cessation

Stop Smoking — We Can Help

Stopping smoking is one of the best things you can do for your health — and your chances of success are significantly higher with professional support. If you don't pay for prescriptions, the process is entirely free.

⏱️ What Happens When You Stop

  • 20 minutes: Blood pressure and heart rate return to normal
  • 8 hours: Carbon monoxide levels halve; oxygen levels normalise
  • 48 hours: All carbon monoxide cleared; taste and smell improve
  • 72 hours: Breathing becomes easier; energy levels increase
  • 1 month: Skin, lungs and circulation begin to improve
  • 1 year: Risk of heart attack halved vs a smoker
  • 10 years: Risk of lung cancer halved vs a smoker

🩺 How We Help at Saluja Clinic

  • Ask reception to refer you to our Quit Smoking Programme
  • Prescriptions for nicotine replacement therapy (NRT) — patches, gum, inhalers
  • Varenicline (Champix) and bupropion available on prescription where appropriate
  • Support from a Healthcare Assistant who knows your medical background
  • Monitoring your health improvements to keep you motivated
✅
Call us on 0208 574 5136 and ask to be referred to the Quit Smoking Programme. One of our Healthcare Assistants will be in contact with you shortly.

Weight Management

Weight & Diet — Personalised Support

Maintaining a healthy weight reduces risk of type 2 diabetes, heart disease, stroke, and several cancers. We offer personalised support — from 1:1 health trainer sessions to nurse-led appointments and referrals to specialist weight management services.

📏 Waist Measurement Matters

BMI gives a useful guide, but waist circumference is an independent marker of metabolic risk. Tummy fat (visceral fat) is more dangerous than fat elsewhere in the body.

  • Men: healthy waist <94cm (37"); increased risk >102cm (40")
  • Women: healthy waist <80cm (32"); increased risk >88cm (35")
  • South Asian men and women may have lower thresholds — speak to your GP
  • 1:1 Health Trainer sessions for lifestyle change and healthy eating
  • Nurse-led weight management appointments
  • Referral to specialist weight loss services where appropriate
  • Prescriptions for weight management medication (Wegovy/Ozempic) if clinically appropriate
  • Small changes matter — even 5–10% weight loss has meaningful health benefits
💡
Book an appointment with one of our nurses or healthcare assistants to discuss weight management. There is no judgement — only practical, evidence-based support.
