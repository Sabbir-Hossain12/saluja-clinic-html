🎗️ NHS Cancer Screening

Breast Screening

The NHS National Breast Screening Programme invites all women aged 50–70 for a free mammogram every three years. Early detection means simpler, more successful treatment.

  • Free mammogram every 3 years, ages 50–70
  • First invitation before your 53rd birthday
  • Under 50 with concerns? Book a GP appointment
  • Saves around 1,400 lives per year in England

🔬 NHS Cancer Screening

Cervical Screening

The smear test detects abnormal cells in the cervix before they become cancer. Our qualified nurses carry out cervical screening at the surgery — quick, private, and vital.

  • Ages 25–49: every 3 years
  • Ages 50–64: every 5 years
  • Appointment takes around 10 minutes
  • Book when you receive your invitation letter

Emergency Contraception

If contraception has failed or you've had unprotected sex, emergency contraception is available free. Effective up to 72 hours — and sometimes 120 hours — after sex.

  • Emergency pill: up to 72 hours after sex
  • IUD (coil): up to 120 hours — most effective option
  • Free even if under 16 · no GP referral needed
  • Call us for an emergency appointment today

Non-Emergency
NHS 111

Available 24/7. For urgent advice when your GP is closed — including concerns about emergency contraception, pregnancy, screening results, or any women's health question.

Life-Threatening Emergency
999

Call 999 immediately for severe pelvic pain, heavy uncontrolled bleeding, suspected ectopic pregnancy, or any life-threatening emergency.
