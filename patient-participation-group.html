What is a PPG?

A Partnership Between
Patients & Practice

A Patient Participation Group (PPG) is a group of patients, carers, and GP practice staff who meet regularly to discuss and improve the services and care provided by the practice. Our PPG is an integral part of how Saluja Clinic operates.

The PPG gives patients a formal voice in how the practice is run, how it communicates, and how services are developed. It is an advisory group — we don't handle individual complaints or medical matters — but we are a trusted channel through which the patient community can engage with the practice leadership.

NHS England guidelines encourage all GP practices to have an active PPG, and at Saluja Clinic we are proud to maintain a group that is genuinely representative of our diverse West London community.

Our PPG is open to all registered patients. Whether you have lived in Southall for decades or have just moved to the area, whether you attend frequently or rarely — your perspective matters and you are welcome.

How the PPG Operates
1
Regular Meetings We meet quarterly at the practice and occasionally virtually. All registered patients are welcome to attend.
2
Formal Agenda Meetings follow a structured agenda shared in advance, covering practice updates, patient feedback themes, and improvement projects.
3
Minutes Published Meeting minutes are recorded and made available to all patients, ensuring full transparency.
4
Practice Response The practice partners respond formally to PPG recommendations, outlining agreed actions and timelines.
5
Virtual Option For those unable to attend in person, we offer a virtual attendance option via video call.
Our Activities

What the PPG Works On

The PPG covers a wide range of topics that make a real difference to patient experience at Saluja Clinic.

Patient Feedback Review
Reviewing anonymised patient feedback, Friends & Family Test results, and complaint themes to identify areas for improvement in our services.
Access & Appointments
Working with the practice team to improve how patients access appointments, reduce waiting times, and ensure fair access for all patient groups.
Practice Communications
Helping improve how the practice communicates with patients — from letters and text messages to signage, the website, and digital services.
Health Promotion
Planning and promoting local health events, screening campaigns, and awareness initiatives that benefit the Southall community.
Patient Surveys
Designing, distributing, and analysing patient surveys to gather structured feedback on specific aspects of care or service delivery.
Community Representation
Ensuring diverse patient groups — including older adults, younger patients, and our multilingual community — have a voice in practice decisions.
Why Join?

What You Gain by
Getting Involved

Membership is voluntary, takes very little of your time, and can make a meaningful difference to how healthcare is delivered in your community.

Have a Real Say Your feedback directly influences decisions about appointment systems, opening times, communication, and new services at Saluja Clinic.
Understand Your NHS Gain insight into how GP practices work, what pressures primary care faces, and how decisions affecting patient care are made.
Represent Your Community Help ensure that the voices of all patients in Southall — especially underrepresented groups — are heard by the practice team.
Flexible Commitment Attend quarterly meetings at a time that suits you, with virtual options available. No minimum attendance requirement — just do what you can.
Early Access to News PPG members receive advance notice of changes to practice services, new clinics, and NHS updates before they are communicated publicly.
No Experience Needed You don't need any medical or healthcare background. All you need is an interest in your local NHS services and a willingness to engage.

Open to Everyone

Who Is Welcome
to Join?

The PPG is open to any patient registered with Saluja Clinic. We actively welcome members who reflect the diversity of our community.

Long-standing Patients Patients who have been with the practice for years and have seen services evolve over time.
Newer Patients & Families Recently registered patients who bring a fresh perspective on access and first impressions.
Carers & Family Representatives Those who support or accompany vulnerable patients and can speak to the carer experience.
Patients with Long-term Conditions Those managing chronic conditions who use the practice regularly and understand its services deeply.
Young Adults & Working Patients Patients in their 20s–40s who can highlight the perspective of those balancing work, family, and healthcare access.
Prefer to get in touch directly? Call us on 0208 574 5136 or email salujaclinic@nhs.net and ask to be added to the PPG mailing list. You can also speak to reception in person at the clinic.
