Our Story

Rooted in the community, committed to you

Saluja Clinic was founded in 1976 and has grown into one of West London's most trusted NHS GP practices. What makes us different is simple: we have always been a family practice in every sense — led by family, serving families.

Dr Rajinder Singh Saluja arrived in England in 1974 and spent over a decade building his reputation across West London before founding The Saluja Clinic in 1986. His son Dr Angad Singh Saluja joined in 2014, bringing with him training from Barts & The London and a passion for modernising patient care without losing the personal touch that defines this practice.

Together, they serve one of London's most diverse communities — patients who value continuity, trust, and a GP who genuinely knows them.

Our GP Partners

The team behind your care

Saluja Clinic is led by two dedicated GP Partners — a father and son whose combined experience spans nearly five decades of NHS general practice.

A family dedicated to West London's health since 1976
🩺 GP Partner & Founding Principal
Dr Rajinder
Singh Saluja
MBBS · Obstetrics & Gynaecology Specialist Interest · GP Since 1974
MBBS Obstetrics & Gynaecology GP Since 1974 Founding Principal 1986

Born in Burma and educated in India, Dr Rajinder Saluja arrived in England in 1974, beginning his distinguished medical career at Luton & Dunstable Hospital. He went on to serve communities across Feltham, Teddington and Southall before founding The Saluja Clinic in 1986 — selected from over 30 candidates. With nearly five decades of service, Dr Rajinder remains a beloved and trusted figure at the heart of this practice.

Languages: Hindi, Urdu, Punjabi, Burmese, Bengali and English

🩺 GP Partner & CQC Registered Manager
Dr Angad
Singh Saluja
BSc (Hons) Sports & Exercise Medicine · MBBS · GP Partner since 2014
BSc Sports & Exercise Medicine MBBS MRCGP FRCGP INT Clinical Lead CQC Registered Manager

Educated at Haberdashers' School and St Bartholomew's & The London Medical School, Dr Angad Saluja joined the practice in 2014, fulfilling a lifelong ambition to work alongside his father. He completed his Foundation Training in Trauma & Emergency Medicine at The Royal London Hospital. Elected as NHS Ealing CCG Governing Board Member and End of Life Clinical Lead, Angad continues to modernise the practice while maintaining the personal care it is known for.

Languages: English, Punjabi
